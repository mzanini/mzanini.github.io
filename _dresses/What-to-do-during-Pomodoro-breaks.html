Since the beginning of 2016 I have been using The Pomodoro Technique® and you know what? I love it!
If you are reading this page, chances are you are already using the technique or are thinking about using it soon.
The Pomodoro Technique® is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo about 30 years ago. The basic concept is that working in short bursts of 25 minutes, separated by 5 minutes breaks, boosts our productivity helping us to overcome procrastination and increase concentration.

What to do during these breaks? Here are some suggestions that I have come up with or collected while surfing the Web:
Always: move away from that screen. During the 5 minutes break we have to give our mind some rest so the basic rule is refrain from doing mind-intensive activities. Pomodoro breaks are also a good time for art, which stimulates your subconscious.
  • Get a cup of coffee
  • Close your eyes, breath deeply
  • Whatch a picture on Google art project
  • Get up and stretch
  • Do breathing exercises
  • Read an inspiring story

  • Write on your journal
  • Listen to one of your favorite songs

  • Look at nature
  • Meditate
